Novels categorised on the basis of
Genre
Romance
Romance novels are primarily based on the romantic relationship between characters. They often explore themes of love, passion, and emotional connections.
Here are some famous romance novels:

"Pride and Prejudice"
by Jane Austen
"The Fault in Our Stars"
by John Green
"Ugly Love"
by Colleen Hoover
"Romeo and Juliet"
by William Shakespeare


Mystery
Mystery novels revolve around solving a puzzle or uncovering the truth behind a crime. They typically feature a detective or amateur sleuth as the protagonist who investigates clues, suspects, and motives. Mystery novels often include elements of suspense, tension, and plot twists.

"The Hound of the Baskervilles"
by Arthur Conan Doyle
"Gone Girl"
by Gillian Flynn
"The Da Vinci Code"
by Dan Brown
"In the Woods"
by Tana French


Science Fiction
Science fiction novels explore speculative concepts, futuristic technologies, and imagined worlds. They often involve scientific or technological advancements and may explore themes related to space exploration, artificial intelligence etc.

"Hyperion"
by Dan Simmons
"The War of the Worlds"
by H.G. Wells
"Brave New World"
by Aldous Huxley
"Neuromancer"
by William Gibson


Fantasy
antasy novels transport readers to imaginary worlds filled with magic, mythical creatures, and epic adventures. They often involve quests, prophecies, and battles between forces of good and evil.

"The Lord of the Rings" by J.R.R. Tolkien
"Harry Potter" series by J.K. Rowling
"The Hobbit" by J.R.R. Tolkien
"Earthsea Cycle" series by Ursula K. Le Guin


Horror
Horror novels aim to evoke feelings of fear, suspense, and terror in readers. They often feature supernatural elements, monsters, or psychological horror.

"Dracula"
by Bram Stoker
"Frankenstein"
by Mary Shelley
"The Exorcist"
by William Peter Blatty
"House of Leaves"
by Mark Z. Danielewski


Thriller
Thriller novels are characterized by their fast-paced, suspenseful plots and high stakes. They often involve intense action, danger, and a sense of urgency as characters navigate through perilous situations.

"The Bourne Identity"
by Robert Ludlum
"The Shining"
by Stephen King
"The Girl on the Train"
by Paula Hawkins
"The Woman in the Window"
by A.J. Finn
